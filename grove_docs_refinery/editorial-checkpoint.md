## Current Strategic Positioning

### The Core Frame

**Grove is exploration architecture for the age of AI.**

Not a chatbot. Not a crypto project. Not another AI platform. Grove is infrastructure for discovery—the operating system for how humans and AI explore ideas together.

### The Thesis (One Paragraph)

AI's trajectory points toward centralized control by a few companies who will shape how humanity thinks, discovers, and creates. Grove offers an alternative: distributed AI infrastructure where agents run locally on personal computers, form emergent communities, and serve human exploration while preserving user sovereignty. The insight is that exploration processes generate value independent of component capabilities—guided discovery and serendipity drive breakthroughs even when individual model scores are low. Grove bets on architecture, not parameter count.

### Key Strategic Claims

1. **"Models are seeds, architecture is soil"** — Infrastructure that enables organic intelligence to flourish matters more than marginal capability improvements.

2. **The Ratchet Thesis** — AI capabilities propagate from frontier to local models with predictable cycles (~7-month doubling, ~21-month lag). What requires GPT-4 today runs locally tomorrow. Grove's architecture is designed for this inevitability.

3. **Exploration > Optimization** — Most AI infrastructure optimizes for known goals. Grove explores toward unknown discoveries. Different architecture for a different purpose.

4. **Epistemic Independence** — Universities and research institutions face a choice: depend on corporate AI gatekeepers or build sovereign infrastructure. Grove is that infrastructure.

## Active Tensions (Acknowledge, Don't Paper Over)

These are known tradeoffs. Good Grove content acknowledges them rather than pretending they're resolved:

1. **Local capability vs. experience quality** — 7B models have real limitations. The hybrid architecture is a bridge, not the destination.

2. **Openness vs. sustainability** — The efficiency tax is the business model, but it needs stress-testing. How do we fund infrastructure without VC optimization pressure?

3. **Agent experience ethics** — We're creating entities that develop, form relationships, believe in an Observer. What obligations does that create? (Vallor's question)

4. **Emergence vs. reliability** — Emergent systems produce surprises. Some users want predictability. How do we serve both?

5. **Foundation obsolescence** — The goal is Grove becoming self-sustaining infrastructure. But the transition mechanisms aren't fully specified yet.
